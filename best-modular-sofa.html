Best Modular Sofas for Comfort, Style, and Flexibility

Publish date: 2024-08-16

With so many more options in terms of shape and positioning than even the best sectional sofas, a modular couch lets you transform your living space pretty much whenever you want to. What to consider when buying a modular sofa

There are a few aspects to consider when getting a modular sofa, including the following.

Price

Modular sofa prices range from affordable to high-end, and while you’ll want to go with what’s appropriate for your budget, do be sure you’re getting what you need. “When purchasing a modular sofa, you should look beyond the price tag and discern the value that the piece brings into the space,” says Artem Kropovinsky, interior design expert and founder of Arsight, an award-winning interior design studio based in New York City. “The couch's essence should resonate with the room's aesthetics and functionalities.”

Size

“In terms of size, don't view the sofa as a standalone entity,” says Kropovinsky. “Instead, see it as a harmonious component of an evolving space, one that allows the room to breathe.” However you arrange the room, though, you will of course have to ensure that your new couch fits in the space in whichever arrangement you prefer. “Measure your space and consider the potential configurations the sofa offers,” advises Brad Smith, interior designer and CEO at Omni Home Ideas.

Configurability

“Modular sofas offer a high level of customization,” points out Mariya Snisar, head of interior design at Renowell. “I’d suggest thinking about how you want to arrange the pieces. Do you prefer a traditional L-shape, a U-shape, or something more unconventional?”

Functionality

“Think about storage, sleeper options, or other features you might need,” suggests Smith. “Are you looking for a lounging space, a formal seating area, or a combination of both?” adds Snisar. “Some modular sofas come with features such as recliners, chaises, or built-in storage. I suggest choosing one that aligns with your lifestyle and requirements.”

Longevity

“Investing in a high-quality modular sofa is essential for longevity,” says Snisar. “Look for reputable brands and check for details like the frame material, cushioning, and stitching.” Smith suggests looking for couches with a sturdy frame, ideally made of solid wood or metal. Kropovinsky, though, points out that, “Longevity is more than rugged durability. A valuable piece ages gracefully over time, akin to a timeless wardrobe piece that remains stylish.”

Materials

“Look for durable and easy-to-clean fabrics or leathers, especially if you have pets or children,” says Smith. The best couches for durability also either feature tightly-woven or performance fabrics.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Are modular couches worth it?

“Modular couches are worth the investment for their exceptional adaptability and space-saving capabilities, providing a versatile seating solution that you can customize to suit various room layouts,” says Snisar. “Their durability and potential for expansion make them a valuable addition to any living space.”

Why are modular sofas so expensive?

“The flexibility, craftsmanship, and often the additional features (like storage or sleepers) can drive up the cost,” admits Smith. Snisar, though, still recommends giving them a try. “I firmly believe that the investment in a modular sofa is well worth it, for their adaptability and space-saving prowess and versatility in function,” she says. “The ability to expand or reconfigure seating arrangements makes them invaluable for hosting guests or adapting to changing room layouts. Their durability and high-quality materials ensure they stand the test of time, making them a smart long-term choice.”

How do modular sofas stay together?

“Modular sofas typically stay together through a combination of connectors and gravity,” explains Snisar. “They often feature specialized hardware, such as clips or brackets, designed to secure the individual modules together. These connectors attach the pieces at key connection points, preventing them from shifting or separating during use. Their weight and the placement of the modules themselves, along with the friction between them and the floor, contribute to the stability of the sofa.”

Are modular and sectional sofas the same?

“Modular and sectional sofas share similarities, but they’re not exactly the same,” says Snisar. “A modular sofa consists of individual pieces that you can arrange and combine in various configurations to suit your room's layout. A sectional sofa, on the flipside, often comes in predefined sections (like L-shape or U-shape). You can’t customize it as extensively as a modular sofa.”
