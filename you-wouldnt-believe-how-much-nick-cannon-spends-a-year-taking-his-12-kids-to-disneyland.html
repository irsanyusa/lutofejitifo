You Won't Believe How Much Nick Cannon Spends a Year Taking His 12 Kids to Disneyland

Publish date: 0001-01-01

TALENTRECAP.COM Entertainment Celebrity

Photo by Jason Mendez/WireImage

Reading Time: 3 minutes

It turned out that Nick Cannon could be Disneyland’s biggest customer each year. The Masked Singer host shared that he spends more than $200,000 taking all his 12 children to Disneyland annually.

Nick Cannon Says He “Probably” Spends $200K a Year to Treat His 12 Kids to Disneyland

During his appearance on New York City’s The Breakfast Club radio show, Cannon stated that he goes to Disneyland “at least once a month.” According to the television personality, he brings his kids there to celebrate special occasions including birthdays and holidays.

“Do you know how much money I spend at Disneyland a year?” Cannon asked the hosts, then receiving a vague response: “A lot.”

“To move around Disney, like, I’m probably spending $200,000 a year at Disneyland,” he confirmed.

Cannon went on and explained that most of that estimated amount goes to paying for hotel rooms, a chaperone to take the kids around the park, and other add-ons alongside the base tickets for the theme park.

The Wild N’ Out star then noted that his expenses are widely different from when he could still get free tickets to the park for hosting it’s Christmas morning parade. At the time, he only had two kids at the time, referring to twins, Moroccan and Monroe, whom he shares with Mariah Carey.

Since having the twins, Cannon has welcomed multiple children. With Brittany Bell, the host is a dad to Golden Sagon, Rise Messiah Cannon, and Powerful Queen. He is also a father to twins Zion Mixolydian and Zillion Heir and Beautiful Zeppelin with Abby De La Rosa. Cannon also welcomed Onyx Ice Cole with LaNisha Cole, and Legendary Love with Bre Tiesi. Lastly, he shares two children with Alyssa Scott, Halo Marie Cannon and Zen, who unfortunately died of brain cancer.

He Reveals Earning $100 Million a Year

While $200,000 a year, it’s fair to say that it’s a trivial chunk of Cannon’s annual earnings. Last May, the media mogul gave a glimpse of his earning capacity, firing back people’s backlash that says he’s a “deadbeat” dad. During his interview with the Los Angeles Times, he stated that $3 million for child support is “not a lot of money.”

“When you think about my lifestyle, I have to generate at least $100 million a year,” he said, later clarifying that he does earn that much.

“Everybody thinks Ryan Seacrest has tons of money. I do everything that he does times 10. Well, not times 10 — times three. Because he does a lot,” he said.

It’s not surprising to know Cannon’s annual salary. For his hosting gig on The Masked Singer alone, he says he’s paid $20 million. He’s also host to Wild ‘N Out, along with a guest stint taking over for Jamie Foxx on Beat Shazam. Cannon is also the co-owner of the Hollywood Hills restaurant, Yamashiro.

He also has current projects including Celebrity Prank Wars, Amazon Freevee’s Counsel Culture, BET’s Future Superstar Tour, the film Hollywood Heist co-starring Alec Baldwin, his daily AMP radio show The Daily Cannon, and the #2HateorNot2Hate podcast. Furthermore, Cannon has his own music imprint, Ncredible. At present, he’s also writing his second romance novel, based on his personal life.
